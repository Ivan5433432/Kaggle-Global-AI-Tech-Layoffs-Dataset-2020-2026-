# Data Analysis Project - Global Tech Layoffs & AI Workforce Transformation (2020–2026)

## Overview

The tech industry has undergone significant shifts since 2020, from pandemic-driven overhiring, to the market correction in 2022–2023, and more recently, AI-driven workforce restructuring.
This project analyzes global tech layoffs between 2020 and 2026 to uncover trends, identify key drivers, and derive business implications.

The goal is to combine data analysis with business interpretation to better understand how technological change, particularly AI adoption, is reshaping workforce dynamics.

---

## Objectives

* Analyze how tech layoffs evolved from 2020 to 2026
* Identify industries, companies, and countries most affected
* Examine the impact of AI-related restructuring
* Evaluate whether layoffs influence stock price changes
* Provide business insights for workforce planning and strategy

---

## Dataset

* Source: Kaggle – Global AI & Tech Layoffs Dataset (2020–2026)
* Records: ~1,800 layoff events
* Key Features:

  * Company and industry information
  * Number of employees laid off
  * Percentage of workforce impacted
  * Layoff reasons
  * Stock price change (1-week)
  * AI-related layoff indicator
  * Geographic location

## Analysis Performed

* Time-series analysis of layoffs trends
* Industry and geographic distribution analysis
* Layoffs by company size
* Correlation analysis using heatmap
* Layoffs vs stock price relationship (scatter plot)
* Layoff reasons analysis
* Department-level impact analysis

---

## Key Insights

* Layoffs increased significantly after 2022, indicating post-pandemic corrections
* Large technology companies account for the majority of layoffs
* AI-driven restructuring is becoming more common
* HR, Marketing, and Operations are frequently impacted departments
* The United States represents the largest share of layoffs globally
* There is no strong relationship between layoffs and short-term stock price changes
* Company size is strongly correlated with layoff volume

---

## Business Implications

* Organizations should prioritize proactive workforce planning
* AI adoption should be accompanied by reskilling initiatives
* Layoffs do not significantly influence short-term market perception
* Flexible workforce strategies can reduce operational disruption
* Companies should avoid aggressive over-hiring during growth periods
* Internal mobility programs can reduce restructuring costs
